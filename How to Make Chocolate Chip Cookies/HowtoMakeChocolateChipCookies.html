How To Make Chocolate Chip Cookies

By Kevin Dang

                                                                                

Every once in a while, we all crave chocolate chip cookies, using this recipe you can easily make delicious cookies. To get started we are going to need to list out the ingredients and the measurements required. Below, you will see what ingredients are required and the measurements. 

Ingredients:

½ cup of white sugar

¾ cup + 2 tablespoons of brown sugar (I used dark brown sugar)

½ cup or 112 grams of unsalted butter melted

½ teaspoon kosher salt

1 whole egg and 1 egg yolk

2 teaspoons vanilla extract

1 ½ cups of all-purpose flour

½ teaspoon baking soda

7 ounces of dark chocolate (60-70 percent cacao) (Note: could also use 7 ounces of chocolate chips).


Materials required:

Measurement spoons/cups for the measurements above

An oven

A medium to large-sized bowl or a stand mixer (Preferred)

A baking tray and parchment paper

Reflection:

The intended audience for this recipe is anyone over the age of fourteen, I chose this because I assumed that for the most part, anyone over that age knows how to use an oven and follow instructions.  

I selected my project by looking at different “how-to” instructions and looking at other recipes. I thought of making chocolate chip cookies because I was craving them and thought why not just make them with my girlfriend over the weekend and document the directions?  

I determined the scope of the project by outlining my thought process like this. In the beginning, I wanted to list out the ingredients because I remember looking at other recipes and thinking “why are the ingredients near the end of the article?” The reason for this is that when shopping for ingredients it is more convenient to have the ingredients in front of you.  

The goal of the article is present clear instructions for baking chocolate chip cookies. There are other parts to the recipe that I decided to limit the details on like chopping the chocolate and melting the butter because I did not want an article that would be too long and allow the reader to get sidetracked.  

I broke down the instructions into two main parts. Part one is making the dough steps (1-15) and the second part is folding the dough and baking the cookies. I did this because I did not want a very long list of instructions for the reader to read through, and if you looked at the recipe there is a forty-five-minute gap between the two parts. 

The formula for each step is one to three sentences describing what the reader will be doing and an image to clarify each step below. The images provide the reader reassurance that they are on the right step when making the dough, I even numbered each step to the corresponding image so the reader knows which image relates to each step. The numbers on the image are labeled in neon green so they are clear to the reader, each image also contains alternative text so the user can read along with the images.

Some feedback I received in my original instructions was that the steps were way too wordy and long, and people claimed that it was hard to visualize each step because some of the steps contained multiple tasks. An example of this would be separating the yolk from the egg in step 10 or the optional cookie-banging method in step 5.

From this feedback, I deleted a lot of my directions in text and relied heavily on using images so each step would be more clear to the user. This cut down on the number of words I used and ultimately made the article easier to follow.  

To mitigate the curse of knowledge I used simple and easy-to-understand words, while also attempting to keep each sentence short and concise so the reader knew what I was doing in each step. To fix step 10 and the optional step 5 I added links to videos because I felt the directions would be easier when demonstrated visually.
